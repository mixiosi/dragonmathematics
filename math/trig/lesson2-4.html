Essential Trigonometric Identities

Master the fundamental relationships between trigonometric functions

Core Identity Groups

Pythagorean Identities

\[ \begin{align*} \sin^2\theta + \cos^2\theta &= 1 \\ 1 + \tan^2\theta &= \sec^2\theta \\ 1 + \cot^2\theta &= \csc^2\theta \end{align*} \]

Reciprocal Identities

\[ \begin{align*} \csc\theta &= \frac{1}{\sin\theta} \\ \sec\theta &= \frac{1}{\cos\theta} \\ \cot\theta &= \frac{1}{\tan\theta} \end{align*} \]

Quotient Identities

\[ \begin{align*} \tan\theta &= \frac{\sin\theta}{\cos\theta} \\ \cot\theta &= \frac{\cos\theta}{\sin\theta} \end{align*} \]

Angle Relationship Identities

Co-function Identities

\[ \begin{align*} \sin\left(\frac{\pi}{2} - \theta\right) &= \cos\theta \\ \cos\left(\frac{\pi}{2} - \theta\right) &= \sin\theta \\ \tan\left(\frac{\pi}{2} - \theta\right) &= \cot\theta \end{align*} \]

Even-Odd Identities

\[ \begin{align*} \sin(-\theta) &= -\sin\theta \\ \cos(-\theta) &= \cos\theta \\ \tan(-\theta) &= -\tan\theta \end{align*} \]

Sum & Difference Identities

Sine Identities
\[ \begin{align*} \sin(A+B) &= \sin A\cos B + \cos A\sin B \\ \sin(A-B) &= \sin A\cos B - \cos A\sin B \end{align*} \]
Cosine Identities
\[ \begin{align*} \cos(A+B) &= \cos A\cos B - \sin A\sin B \\ \cos(A-B) &= \cos A\cos B + \sin A\sin B \end{align*} \]

Practice Exercises

Problem 1: Prove \(1 + \tan^2\theta = \sec^2\theta\)

Start with Pythagorean identity: \[ \sin^2\theta + \cos^2\theta = 1 \] Divide both sides by \(\cos^2\theta\): \[ \tan^2\theta + 1 = \sec^2\theta \]
