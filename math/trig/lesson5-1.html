Parametric Curves

Modeling Complex Motion and Shapes

Fundamental Concepts

Parametric Form

\[ x = f(t),\ y = g(t) \]

Example: Circular motion

\[ x = r\cos t,\ y = r\sin t \]

Conversion to Cartesian

\[ \frac{x^2}{a^2} + \frac{y^2}{b^2} = 1 \Rightarrow \begin{cases} x = a\cos t \\ y = b\sin t \end{cases} \]

Advanced Curves

Lissajous Figures
\[ \begin{cases} x = \sin(at) \\ y = \sin(bt + \phi) \end{cases} \]
Rose Curves
\[ r = a\cos(k\theta) \]

k petals if odd

Cycloid
\[ \begin{cases} x = r(t - \sin t) \\ y = r(1 - \cos t) \end{cases} \]

Motion Analysis

Position Vector
\[ \vec{r}(t) = \langle x(t), y(t) \rangle \]
Velocity Vector
\[ \vec{v}(t) = \langle \frac{dx}{dt}, \frac{dy}{dt} \rangle \]
