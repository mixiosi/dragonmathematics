Simple Harmonic Motion

Modeling Oscillatory Systems with Trigonometry

Fundamental Equations

\[ x(t) = A\cos(\omega t + \phi) \] \[ \omega = \sqrt{\frac{k}{m}} \quad \text{(Angular frequency)} \]

Mass-Spring System

For a spring with constant \( k \) and mass \( m \):

\[ T = 2\pi\sqrt{\frac{m}{k}} \quad \text{(Period)} \]

Example: \( m = 2\text{kg}, k = 8\text{N/m} \)

\[ T = 2\pi\sqrt{\frac{2}{8}} = \pi \text{ seconds} \]

Simple Pendulum

For length \( L \) and gravity \( g \):

\[ T = 2\pi\sqrt{\frac{L}{g}} \]

Example: \( L = 1\text{m}, g = 9.8\text{m/s²} \)

\[ T \approx 2\pi\sqrt{0.102} \approx 2\text{ seconds} \]

Energy Conservation

Kinetic Energy
\[ KE = \frac{1}{2}m\omega^2(A^2 - x^2) \]
Potential Energy
\[ PE = \frac{1}{2}kx^2 \]
Total Energy
\[ E = \frac{1}{2}kA^2 \]

Damped Oscillations

\[ x(t) = Ae^{-\beta t}\cos(\omega_d t + \phi) \]

Where:

  • \( \beta \): Damping coefficient
  • \( \omega_d = \sqrt{\omega_0^2 - \beta^2} \): Damped frequency

Practice Exercise

A 0.5kg mass oscillates with amplitude 0.2m and period 1.5s. Calculate:
  1. Spring constant \( k \)
  2. Maximum velocity

1. Using \( T = 2\pi\sqrt{m/k} \):

\[ k = \frac{4\pi^2 m}{T^2} = \frac{4\pi^2(0.5)}{2.25} \approx 8.77\text{N/m} \]

2. Maximum velocity \( v_{max} = A\omega \):

\[ \omega = \frac{2\pi}{T} \approx 4.19\text{rad/s} \] \[ v_{max} = 0.2 \times 4.19 \approx 0.84\text{m/s} \]
