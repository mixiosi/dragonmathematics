Polar Coordinates

Mastering Circular and Rotational Systems

Coordinate Conversion

Polar to Cartesian

\[ x = r\cos\theta \] \[ y = r\sin\theta \]

Cartesian to Polar

\[ r = \sqrt{x^2 + y^2} \] \[ \theta = \arctan\left(\frac{y}{x}\right) \]

Common Polar Graphs

Circle
\[ r = 2a\cos\theta \]

Radius a, centered at (a,0)

Cardioid
\[ r = a(1 + \cos\theta) \]

Heart-shaped curve

Rose Curve
\[ r = a\cos(n\theta) \]

n petals if n is odd

Plotting Exercise

Plot (3, π/4)
  1. Rotate 45° from x-axis
  2. Move 3 units from origin
  3. Equivalent Cartesian coordinates:
    4. \[ x = 3\cos(\pi/4) \approx 2.12 \] \[ y = 3\sin(\pi/4) \approx 2.12 \]
Plot (-2, 5π/6)
  1. Rotate 150° from x-axis
  2. Move 2 units in opposite direction
  3. Equivalent Cartesian coordinates:
    4. \[ x = -2\cos(5\pi/6) \approx 1.73 \] \[ y = -2\sin(5\pi/6) \approx -1 \]

Practice Problems

Convert between coordinate systems:
  1. Cartesian (4, -3) to Polar
  2. Polar (5, 2π/3) to Cartesian

1. Polar coordinates:

\[ r = \sqrt{4^2 + (-3)^2} = 5 \] \[ \theta = \arctan(-3/4) \approx -0.64\text{rad} \ (360° - 36.87° = 323.13°) \]

2. Cartesian coordinates:

\[ x = 5\cos(2\pi/3) = -2.5 \] \[ y = 5\sin(2\pi/3) \approx 4.33 \]
