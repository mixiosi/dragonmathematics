Trigonometric Functions of Any Angle

Extend trigonometric ratios to all quadrants using reference angles

Core Concepts

Figure 1: Standard Quadrant System

Reference Angles

Definition

The acute angle between the terminal side and x-axis:

\[ \theta_{\text{ref}} = \begin{cases} \theta & \text{if } \theta \in \text{QI} \\ 180^\circ - \theta & \text{if } \theta \in \text{QII} \\ \theta - 180^\circ & \text{if } \theta \in \text{QIII} \\ 360^\circ - \theta & \text{if } \theta \in \text{QIV} \end{cases} \]

Examples
  • \(210^\circ \rightarrow 30^\circ\) (QIII)
  • \(135^\circ \rightarrow 45^\circ\) (QII)
  • \(300^\circ \rightarrow 60^\circ\) (QIV)

Quadrant Sign Rules

Quadrant sin cos tan
I + + +
II + - -
III - - +
IV - + -
Mnemonic: "All Students Take Calculus"
Quadrants I-IV: All, Sine, Tangent, Cosine positive respectively

Unit Circle Relationships

Primary Functions

For any angle \(\theta\):

\[ \sin\theta = y \quad \cos\theta = x \quad \tan\theta = \frac{y}{x} \]

Reciprocal Functions

\[ \csc\theta = \frac{1}{\sin\theta} \quad \sec\theta = \frac{1}{\cos\theta} \quad \cot\theta = \frac{1}{\tan\theta} \]

Problem: Find \(\sin(225^\circ)\) and \(\tan(225^\circ)\)

  • Determine quadrant:
    • 225¬∞ is in Quadrant III
  • Calculate reference angle: \[ 225¬∞ - 180¬∞ = 45¬∞ \]
  • Apply quadrant signs: \[ \sin(225¬∞) = -\sin(45¬∞) = -\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} \] \[ \tan(225¬∞) = +\tan(45¬∞) = 1 \]

    Find \(\cos(150^\circ)\) and \(\csc(300^\circ)\)
    • 150¬∞ (QII): \[ \cos(150¬∞) = -\cos(30¬∞) = -\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \]
    • 300¬∞ (QIV): \[ \csc(300¬∞) = \frac{1}{-\sin(60¬∞)} = -\frac{2}{\sqrt{3}} = -\frac{2\sqrt{3}}{3} \]
