Trigonometric Functions of Acute Angles

Master the fundamental ratios and their applications in right triangles

Core Trigonometric Ratios

Sine (sinθ)

\[ \sin(\theta) = \frac{\text{Opposite}}{\text{Hypotenuse}} \]

Cosine (cosθ)

\[ \cos(\theta) = \frac{\text{Adjacent}}{\text{Hypotenuse}} \]

Tangent (tanθ)

\[ \tan(\theta) = \frac{\text{Opposite}}{\text{Adjacent}} \]

Special Triangles

30°-60°-90° Triangle (Ratio 1 : √3 : 2)

45°-45°-90° Triangle (Ratio 1 : 1 : √2)

Standard Trigonometric Values

Angle (θ) sin(θ) cos(θ) tan(θ)
30° \(\frac{1}{2}\) \(\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\) \(\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}\)
45° \(\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}\) \(\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}\) 1
60° \(\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\) \(\frac{1}{2}\) \(\sqrt{3}\)

Calculator Usage Tips

  • Always verify angle mode (DEG/RAD)
  • Use parenthesis for complex inputs
  • Remember: \(\tan(90^\circ)\) is undefined

Unit Circle Fundamentals

Key Relationships
  • \(\sin\theta = y\text{-coordinate}\)
  • \(\cos\theta = x\text{-coordinate}\)
  • \(\tan\theta = \frac{y}{x}\)

Practice Exercises

Problem 1: Height Calculation

A ladder leaning against a wall forms a 60° angle with the ground. If the ladder is 10m long, how high up the wall does it reach?

Using sine: \[ \sin(60°) = \frac{\text{Height}}{10} \Rightarrow \text{Height} = 10 \times \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \approx 8.66\text{m} \]
