Mastering Phase Shifts & Curve Fitting

Transform and model periodic phenomena with precision

General Sinusoidal Form

\[ y = A\sin(Bx + C) + D \quad \text{or} \quad y = A\cos(Bx + C) + D \]
Parameter Effect Formula
Amplitude (A) Vertical stretch/compression \( |A| = \text{Peak Value} \)
Period (B) Horizontal stretch/compression \( \frac{2\pi}{|B|} \)
Phase Shift (C) Horizontal displacement \( -\frac{C}{B} \)
Vertical Shift (D) Midline adjustment Average value

Figure 1: Phase Shift Visualization

Worked Example: \( y = 3\cos(2x - \frac{\pi}{2}) + 1 \)

Amplitude

\( A = 3 \)
Oscillates between -2 and 4

Period

\( \frac{2\pi}{2} = \pi \)

Phase Shift

\( \frac{\pi}{4} \) right

\[ \text{Midline: } y = 1 \]

Curve Fitting Process

  1. Determine midline (D) from data average
  2. Calculate amplitude (A) from max/min deviations
  3. Identify period (B) from cycle duration
  4. Compute phase shift (C) to align starting point
  5. Verify with key points

Practice Exercise

Analyze \( y = -2\sin(0.5x + \pi) + 3 \)
  • Amplitude: 2 (flipped vertically)
  • Period: \( 4\pi \)
  • Phase Shift: \( 2\pi \) left
  • Midline: \( y = 3 \)
  • Range: [1, 5]
